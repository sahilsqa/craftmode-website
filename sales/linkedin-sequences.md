## General Best Practices

### Do's
- ✅ Send between 9am-11am or 2pm-4pm (recipient's timezone)
- ✅ Personalize at least one specific detail per message
- ✅ Keep messages under 100 words when possible
- ✅ Make the ask clear and low-friction ("worth a quick chat?")
- ✅ Use voice notes for follow-ups (stands out in inbox)

### Don'ts
- ❌ Never send connection request + pitch in same message
- ❌ Don't use LinkedIn automation tools (risk of ban)
- ❌ Avoid industry jargon or buzzwords
- ❌ Don't follow up more than twice
- ❌ Never argue with a "no thanks"

### Subject Line (for InMail)
- "Quick question about {{CompanyName}}'s website"
- "Saw {{CompanyName}}'s growth—congrats!"
- "Referral ask: know anyone who needs a website?"

### Response Templates (When They Reply)

**Positive response:**
> Thanks {{FirstName}}! Happy to jump on a quick call. What does your calendar look like this week or next? I'm flexible.

**"Not right now":**
> Totally understand—timing is everything. I'll check back in a few months if that's okay. Mind if I stay connected here?

**"We have a developer":**
> Makes sense! Most of our clients have dev teams but need something *fast* without pulling them off product work. But if you're all set, no worries at all.
